🏠 Which type of utility bill do you have?

Select the one that matches your bill. If you already have rooftop solar, your bill shows both consumption and export generation — we'll use your solar panel size to estimate your gross home usage.

Without solar (one bar)

With solar (two bars)

How we'll calculate your gross usage: Since your bill's "electricity you used" is net of what your solar system powered directly, we'll estimate your gross home usage as:

gross usage = net usage + (estimated solar generation − exported solar)

We'll estimate your solar generation from the Solar Panel Capacity (kW) you entered above. Please ensure that field is filled in.

📊 How to Find Your Daily Average Usage (SCE Customers)

If you have Southern California Edison (SCE), open the "Your past and current electricity usage" section of your bill and look at "Your daily average electricity usage (kWh)". Copy the number next to "This year:" into the field above.

In this example, the annual daily average is 24.10 kWh. 💡 Click the image to view full size.

If you have Southern California Edison (SCE) and your bill shows export generation, open "Your past and current electricity usage" → "Your daily average electricity usage (kWh)". You'll find two "This year:" values — one under Electricity Usage (your net consumption) and one under Export Generation (exported solar). Copy each into the matching field above.

In this example, Electricity Usage "This year: 23.20" and Export Generation "This year: 12.17". 💡 Click the image to view full size.

📊 How to Find Your Monthly Usage (SCE Customers)

If you have Southern California Edison (SCE), open the "Your past and current electricity usage" section of your bill. At the top you'll see "Total electricity you used this month in kWh" — that's the total for the most recent billing period. Below it, the bar chart shows your daily average (kWh/day) for each of the last 12 months — read each bar's height and type it into the matching month field above.

In this example, the total for the month is 699 kWh, and each bar in the chart below (~23 kWh/day for Aug '25) is what you enter for that month. 💡 Click the image to view full size.

If you have Southern California Edison (SCE) and your bill shows export generation, open "Your past and current electricity usage". At the top you'll see two totals for this month: "Total electricity you used this month in kWh" (net consumption) and "Total electricity you exported this month in kWh". Below, the bar chart shows your daily averages for each month — the darker "Consumption" bar and the lighter "Export Generation" bar. Read each bar's height and enter them in the matching month fields above.

In this example, used = 695 kWh and exported = 365 kWh for the billing period. Read the darker bars for Consumption and the lighter bars for Export Generation. 💡 Click the image to view full size.
